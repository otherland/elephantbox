FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions about Elephant Box.

Q:
Why stainless steel?
March 15, 2015

There is much talk in the press about the dangers of plastics. Stainless steel ranks top in terms of safest containers for food. Plastics leak chemicals. Our products are not manufactured using tin or aluminum, or cheaply mass produced. They are heavy-duty and meant to last for years. They are made of 202 grade stainless steels. Aluminium is often marketed as a cheap alternative to plastic. Aluminium is no stranger to the safety debate due to the link to Alzheimer’s. Most aluminium water bottles contain an interior plastic coating to protect the contents from exposure, but this leaves you with a plastic bottle (see above). The coating can also degrade over time, particularly if your bottle gets dented. Stainless steel may be marginally heavier but it’s also that bit tougher than aluminium and therefore less prone to scratches and dents.

Q:
Is the Elephant Box leak proof?
March 31, 2015

Our boxes are a modern version of the traditional Indian latch tiffin. Tiffins have been around for 50 years and the design has not changed for a reason - it just works. Simple. Beautiful. Functional. Latch tiffins were never designed to be 100% leak-proof. When latched, liquids will only spill out if the tiffin is left on its side and then it is generally a small dribble.
